TrainABA Verified

The simplest structured approach to developing exam-ready credential applicants is TrainABA Verified. Each learner has a competency list with criteria that must be met and, fittingly, "verified" by the supervisor. The supervisor looks at the performance and makes a single choice (Verify or not). When verified, evidence of learning is attached. The non-verifiable attempts are not attached. What is a performance measure? Consider each item on the competency task list as a response class. The TrainABA Framework provides a list of performance measures in that response class. The learner sees her task list progress and tackles one at a time. When ready, she submits work to her supervisor. The supervisor looks at, determines whether the performance criteria is met, and clicks "Verify" for that response class (task list item). Immediately, the supervisor and learner have an updated task list with attached evidence. When the learner passes the board exam (any level), the learner moves on. What form do performance measures take? They are usually in the form of a written submission, observed via role-play or iin vivo, or assessed via interview. In one click, supervisors "Verify" with evidence attached. Over time, TrainABA Verified collects longitudinal data and runs statistical analyses to validate that response-class items actually load on the corresponding task list items. The system will adjust according to data and shed light on a better technology for developing exam-ready credential applicants that we can all use (company, university, country, and individual agnostic, just science). It's easy to say qualitatively that folks who show evidence at X target perform at X level on the credential exam but there are other variables that will confound, puzzle, tease, and delight us in our search for an evidence-based credential-applicant development technology.
